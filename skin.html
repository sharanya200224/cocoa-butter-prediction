Cocoa Butter Processing Methods

Discover the journey from cocoa beans to premium cocoa butter through traditional and modern techniques

From Bean to Butter: The 8-Step Process

Learn how cocoa beans are transformed into the rich, luxurious cocoa butter used in cosmetics and chocolate

Harvesting & Collecting

Cocoa pods are harvested by hand when fully ripe. Farmers carefully cut the pods from trees without damaging the flower cushions, ensuring future harvests. The pods are then opened to extract the cocoa beans surrounded by sweet white pulp.

Harvest Season

Main harvest: Oct-Feb, Mid-crop: May-Aug

Yield

30-40 pods per tree annually

Quality Check

Only ripe, undamaged pods selected

Cleaning & Sorting

Fresh cocoa beans are cleaned to remove debris, stones, and damaged beans. They're sorted by size and quality using vibrating screens and optical sorters. This critical step ensures only premium beans continue in the process.

Debris Removal

Sticks, leaves, stones removed

Quality Sorting

Defective beans discarded

Size Grading

Uniform beans for even processing

Roasting

Beans are roasted at 120-140°C for 20-40 minutes to develop flavor and aroma. This critical step reduces moisture, loosens shells, and triggers Maillard reactions that create complex flavor compounds.

Temperature

120-140°C (248-284°F)

Duration

20-40 minutes

Flavor Development

Maillard reactions create complex flavors

Crushing & Winnowing

Roasted beans are cracked to separate the brittle shells from the nibs (cocoa meat). Winnowing uses air streams to blow away lighter shells while heavier nibs fall through. This produces pure cocoa nibs ready for grinding.

Shell Removal

95-99% efficiency

Nib Size

3-8mm particles

Quality Control

Infrared sorting for purity

Grinding Cocoa Mass

Cocoa nibs are ground into a thick paste called cocoa liquor or cocoa mass. Friction from grinding stones generates heat (50-70°C), melting cocoa butter and creating a smooth liquid. This takes 18-72 hours depending on desired fineness.

Temperature

50-70°C (122-158°F)

Particle Size

15-30 microns

Duration

18-72 hours

Pressing Cocoa Butter

Cocoa liquor is pressed under high pressure (400-500 bar) to separate cocoa butter from cocoa solids. Hydraulic presses extract 40-50% of the liquor as butter. The remaining solids form cocoa cakes which are ground into powder.

Pressure

400-500 bar

Yield

40-50% butter extraction

Cocoa Cake

10-12% residual fat content

Filtration & Purification

Raw cocoa butter is filtered through fine screens (10-25μm) to remove any solid particles. For premium cosmetic-grade butter, additional purification steps may include activated carbon filtration to remove impurities and odors.

Filtration Level

10-25 micron filters

Deodorization

Steam treatment at 200-220°C

Impurity Removal

Activated carbon filtration

Tempering & Cooling

Butter is slowly cooled under controlled conditions (18-20°C) to promote proper crystallization. This tempering process stabilizes the butter's polymorphic structure, ensuring consistent melting properties and texture.

Cooling Rate

2-3°C per minute

Temperature

18-20°C (64-68°F)

Crystallization

Form V crystals for stability
